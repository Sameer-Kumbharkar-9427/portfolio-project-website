Identifying key customer trends and patterns of bike buyers

Project Summary ▼
This project focuses on analyzing the Bike Buyers Dataset to identify key customer trends and patterns. The final output is an interactive Excel Dashboard that visualizes insights about bike purchases based on customer demographics. The dashboard allows users to filter data dynamically using slicers for better decision-making.
Tools & Technologies Used ▼

Microsoft Excel

  • Pivot Tables & Pivot Charts
  • Data Cleaning (Find & Replace, Conditional Formatting)
  • Formulas (IF statements for categorization)
  • Data Visualization (Bar Chart, Line Chart, Slicers)

Bike buyers dataset
Data Description ▼

The dataset contains the following key attributes:

  • Customer Demographics – Age, Gender, Marital Status, Region, Education
  • Financial Information – Annual Income
  • Commute Details – Distance from home to work
  • Purchase Decision – Whether the customer bought a bike (Yes/No)
Data Cleaning & Preparation ▼

To ensure data consistency and accuracy, the following steps were performed:

  • Removed duplicate records to eliminate redundant data.
  • Standardized Gender & Marital Status columns using Find & Replace to ensure uniformity.
  • Formatted "Income" column as currency for better readability.
  • Created an "Age Bracket" column using the formula:
    • 
        =IF(L15>54,"Old",IF(L15>=31,"Middle Age",IF(L15<31,"Adolescent","Invalid")))

    This categorized customers into Adolescent, Middle Age, and Old groups.

Conclusion ▼
  • Most bike buyers are middle-aged and have higher income, especially males.

  • People living closer (0–1 miles) to their destination are more likely to purchase bikes.

  • Understanding customer age, income, and commute distance can help improve marketing and sales strategies.
Key Insights ▼
  • Among both genders, bike purchasers earn more on average than non-buyers. Males who purchased bikes had the highest average income overall.

  • Middle-aged customers are the biggest group of bike buyers. Adolescents and older adults buy fewer bikes compared to middle-aged customers.

  • Most purchases happen for short commutes (0-1 miles). As commute distance increases, bike purchases decrease significantly.
